Marine Biodiversity Workshop: from the Field to the Cloud

Marine Biodiversity Workshop: from the Field to the Cloud

1 Overview

  • Project: Pole-to-Pole (P2P) Marine Biodiversity Observation Network (MBON) & Americas Global Earth Observation System of Systems (AmeriGEOSS)
  • Location: Praia do Cabelo Gordo, São Sebastião, Brasil
  • Dates: August 6-10, 2018

Figure 1.1: Locations of the workshop in São José dos Campos and CEBIMar.

1.1 Objectives

This workshop will engage participants in marine biodiversity activities in the field and behind the computer that promote a community of best practices.

  1. Collect field data across multiple habitats: rocky intertidal and sandy beaches habitats (SARCE).
  2. Manipulate tabular and spatial data for standardized data formats, such as Darwin Core, while controlling for quality
  3. Populate datasets to OBIS, using tools for data discovery, analysis, and sharing
  4. Build species distribution models by correlating satellite derived environmental data with biological observations to predict across seascapes
  5. Learn how to use data science tools (R, Rmarkdown, Github) for producing reproducible research documents with interactive visualizations onto the web.
